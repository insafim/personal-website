## Why calibration matters here

Prompt tuning improves accuracy on a downstream task, but a model that becomes
*more confident* about its wrong answers is often worse downstream than the
zero-shot baseline - especially in selective-prediction or human-in-the-loop
settings. Calibration is the missing axis the accuracy-only metric hides.

## What the dual-regularization does

The mean-variance margin loss penalizes confident wrong predictions; the text
moment-matching term keeps the tuned prompt embedding close to the original CLIP
text distribution, which is the part of the model that was actually well-calibrated
to begin with. Together they reduce ECE without sacrificing the accuracy lift.
